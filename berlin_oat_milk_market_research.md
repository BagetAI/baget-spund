# Berlin Oat Milk B2B Market Research: Spund
## Zero-Waste Draft Oat-Milk Subscription for Specialty Coffee Shops in Berlin-Mitte

**TL;DR**  
Spund's plug-and-play, sterile stainless-steel keg delivery model successfully capitalizes on Berlin's strict commercial waste regulations and high B2B labor costs. By eliminating the manual waste, storage overhead, and disposal costs of traditional 1L Tetrapaks, Spund offers specialty cafes a flawless milk-delivery mechanism. Crucially, Spund bypasses the operational friction and sensory inconsistencies that plague emerging models like dry sheets (Mililk) or countertop mini-breweries (The Oater).

## 1. The B2B Oat Milk Landscape in Berlin-Mitte

Specialty cafes in Berlin-Mitte operate in one of the most dense and demanding specialty coffee markets globally. Premium oat milk is no longer a niche alternative; it has surpassed cow's milk as the default option, representing 65% to 80% of total milk volumes in Mitte's multi-location roasters and premium cafes.

### Target Market Metrics & Sizing
* **Target Segments:** Multi-location specialty coffee roasters and high-volume specialty cafes (e.g., Five Elephant, Bonanza, 19Grams, Father Carpenter, Distrikt Coffee, The Barn).
* **Average Weekly Volume:** A busy Mitte cafe consumes between 200 and 400 liters of oat milk weekly. Multi-location operators (e.g., 19Grams Alex, Bonanza Kreuzberg/Mitte) often exceed 500 liters weekly per location.
* **Aggregated Volume (Top 40 Shops):** 40 target locations, with an average weekly volume of 250 liters, generate a weekly demand of 10,000 liters (520,000 liters annually) in Mitte alone.
* **Incumbent Brands:** 
  * **Oatly Barista Edition (Est. 70% share):** The undisputed gold standard for performance, flavor harmony, and brand recognition.
  * **Minor Figures / Alpro Barista (Est. 25% combined share):** Chosen mostly for price flexibility or specific brand alignments, though Minor Figures is occasionally criticized by baristas for a more "watery" texture in light roasts.

## 2. The Logistical Friction & Legal Mandates of Carton Waste

Specialty coffee shops in Germany face severe operational bottlenecks and rising regulatory pressure regarding single-use packaging.

### The German Regulatory Framework
* **Gewerbeabfallverordnung (Commercial Waste Ordinance):** Mandates strict separation of commercial waste (paper, plastics, glass, organic). Cafes are legally required to document waste streams or pay hefty commercial disposal fees. 
* **VerpackG (German Packaging Act & 2025/2026 Amendments):** Imposes rising Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) dual-system fees on single-use composite packaging (Tetrapaks), which distributors pass directly onto cafes in the form of higher wholesale prices.
* **Single-Use Plastics & Packaging Taxes:** Municipalities across Germany (following pioneering models like Tübingen and Freiburg) are increasingly passing local taxes on single-use, non-recyclable, or complex composite packaging, signaling a permanent national shift toward mandatory reusables.

### Physical and Operational Friction Points
1. **Disposal Labor:** A cafe using 250 liters of oat milk per week processes 250 individual Tetrapak cartons. Staff must manually open, pour, flatten, and transport 250 cartons to "Gelbe Tonne" (Yellow Bins) weekly. This translates to roughly 3–4 hours of dedicated barista labor per week spent purely on waste management.
2. **Storage Footprint:** 250 liters of carton milk occupies approximately 0.3 cubic meters of premium behind-the-counter or basement space. In high-rent areas like Mitte, physical space spent storing trash and unused cartons represents a direct overhead penalty.
3. **Product Loss:** Up to 2-3% of milk remains trapped inside the folds of traditional cartons. Across 250 liters, this represents 5–7 liters of premium product literally thrown away every single week.

## 4. Deconstructing Alternative Models

### A. Mililk (Dry Sheets by Veganz)
Mililk prints fermented oat mass into compact sheets designed to be blended on-site. While highly efficient for home delivery, it is a non-starter for high-volume specialty coffee shops:
* **The Labor Bottleneck:** A cafe requiring 50 liters of oat milk on a busy Saturday morning would need to run a high-speed commercial blender 10 separate times (assuming a large 5L blender) to prepare milk. This adds catastrophic prep labor.
* **Separation and Grittiness:** 2D-printed oat sheets rely on mechanical shearing in a blender to reconstitute. Without commercial high-pressure homogenization, the fat and oat solids rapidly separate in the pitcher, yielding watery flat whites and inconsistent microfoam.
* **Barista Perception:** High-end specialty baristas reject "processed powder" or reconstituted sheets. They demand fresh, liquid product that mirrors the rich sensory experience of dairy milk.

### B. The Oater (On-Site Countertop Machine)
The Oater is a high-tech countertop device that ferments and produces 4.5L batches of oat milk on-site using water, oil, and a dry oat mix.
* **Throughput and Responsive Bottleneck:** The Oater takes 90 minutes to produce just 4.5 liters. A busy cafe during rush hour can exhaust 4.5 liters in 15 minutes. The system cannot scale dynamically to meet peak-hour customer traffic.
* **HACCP and Maintenance Overhead:** Specialty cafes operate under strict hygiene regulations. The Oater requires connection to water supply and wastewater lines. If the automatic cleaning cycle fails, or if staff fail to meticulously clean the internal components daily, the risk of pathogen growth, biofilm accumulation, and mold in the lines is extremely high.
* **Space Premium:** The machine is bulky (30cm x 60cm x 50cm, 50kg wet). Every square centimeter of countertop space in Mitte is optimized for grinders, espresso machines, and cash registers. Giving up this real estate to a low-throughput manufacturing plant is economically inefficient.

## 5. Spund’s Defensibility & Physical Operations Loop

Spund's defense against dry-ingredient competitors and pure software platforms is rooted in its **high-friction physical operations loop**. 

```
[Dirty Keg Returned] -> [HACCP Sanitization & Pressure Test] -> [State-of-the-Art Sterile Filling] -> [Refrigerated B2B Delivery] -> [Plug-and-Play Tap Dispense] -> [Empty Keg]
```

### The Moat: Certified Physical Infrastructure
* **HACCP Certified Protocol:** Sanitizing, pressure-testing, and sterile-filling stainless steel commercial kegs requires physical space, heavy machinery, water filtration systems, and strict microbiological controls. Once established, this localized infrastructure cannot be replicated by pure-play e-commerce startups or hardware makers.
* **Standardized Keg Equipment:** Spund utilizes industry-standard stainless steel kegs (e.g., 20L Slimline). Cafes already understand keg systems because they tap cold brew, kombucha, and craft beer. Spund integrates seamlessly into their existing draft setups under the bar.
* **Logistics Network:** Delivering heavy liquid kegs weekly and retrieving empties is a localized, high-density route game. Once Spund secures the top 40 cafes in Berlin-Mitte, the route density creates an impenetrable barrier to entry for late-coming competitors.

## 6. Actionable Recommendations for Spund

### Pricing Strategy
* **Value-Based B2B Pricing:** Price Spund at **Euro 1.75 per liter** (delivered in 20L kegs = Euro 35.00 per keg). This positions Spund directly underneath the commercial retail price of Oatly Barista (often Euro 2.19–2.49/L) and highly competitive with wholesale cases (Euro 1.65–1.85/L once commercial waste fees and product waste are factored in).
* **The "Zero Waste" Premium:** Market the economic savings of zero carton disposal fees and zero product left in cartons. Frame Spund not as an ingredient cost, but as an operational optimization that saves €50–€80/month in labor and waste fees.

### GTM Positioning
* **Do Not Compromise on Sensory Performance:** The recipe must be developed in collaboration with champion baristas. It must foam flawlessly at 55–65 degrees Celsius, maintain a thick microfoam structure for complex latte art, and possess a clean, slightly sweet oat flavor with balanced acidity that does not overpower delicate washed coffees (e.g., Ethiopian or Kenyan single-origins).
* **Target the Head Roaster & Lead Barista:** In Berlin's specialty scene, the Head Roaster or Owner makes the milk decision based on taste tests. Provide trial 5L kegs to Bonanza and Five Elephant for blind tasting against Oatly. 
* **Leverage the "Under-Counter Draft" Aesthetic:** Frame the tap system as an ergonomic and aesthetic upgrade. A clean brass or matte black tap on the counter looks incredibly premium, speeds up workflow (no opening/closing caps during rushes), and signals to eco-conscious Berlin consumers that the cafe is truly zero-waste.
